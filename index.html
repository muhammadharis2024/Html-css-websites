Our Achievements

Apollo 11

Apollo 11 was the American spaceflight that first landed humans on the Moon. Commander Neil Armstrong and Lunar Module Pilot Buzz Aldrin landed the Apollo Lunar Module Eagle on July 20, 1969

Hubble Space Telescope

Launched on April 24, 1990 aboard the space shuttle Discovery, Hubble orbits roughly 340 miles (547 km) above Earth's surface. It completes 15 orbits per day, circling Earth approximately once every 95 minutes.

James Webb Space Telescope

The James Webb Space Telescope is a space telescope designed to conduct infrared astronomy. Its high-resolution and high-sensitivity instruments allow it to view objects too old, distant, or faint for the Hubble Space Telescope.
